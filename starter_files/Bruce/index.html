Copper Gold Ratio Signal

The Copper Gold ratio is commonly referred to as an indicator of the macro economic environemnt. Using the price relationship of these two metals (copper price / gold price) we can see that higher values in the ratio would potentially indicate econcomic growth where lower values would indicate economic contraction. In building upon this idea I explored whether there was a relationship between the pricing of the S&P 500 and the Copper Gold ration (CGr) and whether or not we could statistically find signals for buying and selling the S&P 500 based on the relationship. The original analysis invovled a data visualization to see if changes in the CGr occurred ahead of market moves in the S&P 500. The conclusion was that there were often movements in the ratio ahead of market moves. The next step was to explore the numbers a bit deeper to filter out noise and potentially identify a true signal of when to buy and or sell the S&P. I examined the correlation of the S&P 500 daily price changes to the CGr daily change and mapped out these over time in relationship to the their standard deviation over a 55 day rolling period. The chart above contains "signals" for when the correlation was above or below two standard deviations. The rest of the noise was filtered out.
